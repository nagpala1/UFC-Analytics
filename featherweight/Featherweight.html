FEATHERWEIGHT

    A new king has been born, an old one has been replaced, and the rest has pretty much stayed the same. Even though he is no longer present, Mcgregor's finger prints are still all over this division..

  • (C): Max Holloway

    17-3: Holloway just gets better and better with each and every fight. Given his young age and willingness to compete, the 145 division may have it's new long-term king.

  • 1: Jose Aldo

    26-3: Losing 2 of his last 3 fights, Aldo's aura of invincibility is history. He is still miles above everyone else, but he definitely is not getting any younger.

  • 2: Frankie Edgar

    21-5: Clearly the third best fighter at 145, Edgar is bound to recieve the next title-shot. If he wins, his first title defense could be against Jose Aldo. Isn't that unfortunate..?

  • 3: Ricardo Lamas

    17-5: Lamas has been in the top 5 for quite some time now. If all of the pieces fall in the right place, he just may recieve another title shot soon.

  • 4: Cub Swanson

    25-7: Swanson has a tendency to get hit.. a lot. Eventually, that chin is going to crack from all of the wear-and-tear it has recieved over the years.

  • 5: Chan Sung Jung

    14-4: There's a reason why Chan is called "Korean Zombie", and it's because he loves to get hit and keep moving forward. Entertaining yes, but can that win him a title?

  • 6: Anthony Pettis

    19-6: No high-level fighter has looked worse since the USADA era than Anthony Pettis. Was he truly on 'PEDS', or is the competition he is facing now just too stiff?

  • 7: Jeremy Stephens

    25-13: WHO THE FOOK IS THAT GUY?

  • 8: Yair Rodriquez

    11-2: Flashy striker with a devastating kicking game. Other than that, Yair doesn't really offer much.

  • 9: Brian Ortega

    11-0: Unbeaten in his professional mixed-martial-arts career, Ortega could very well be the next big thing at 145.
