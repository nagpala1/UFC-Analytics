LIGHT HEAVYWEIGHT

    From Chuck Liddel to Rampage Jackson, to Rashad Evans to Lyoto Machida, to Shogun Hua to Jon Jones and Daniel Cormier, the lightheavy weight division will forever go down in history as the most iconic. Although the depth at 205 is currently slim, the top 3 fighters - Cormier, Jones and Gus - may be as strong as any other top 3 in the entire UFC.

  • (C): Daniel Cormier

    19-1: There is one thing left to accomplish for Cormier, and it's no easy task: defeat the best fighter that has ever graced the octagon, Jon Jones..

  • 1: Jon Jones

    22-1: For anybody who is a regular viewer of MMA, we all know Jones' true record actually stands at 23-0. If the drugs are truly behind him, he can finally re-continue his journey for the 'greatest of all time' title.

  • 2: Alexander Gustafsson

    18-4: Gustafsson is as popular as anyone inside and outside the octagon. If he can improve his striking defense, he just may be able to best Jones and Cormier in a rematch

  • 3: Jimi Manuwa

    17-2: The heaviest hitter at 205, Manuwa is an absolute savage. However, his chin still remains questionable, and so does his gas tank.

  • 4: Glover Teixeira

    26-6: Glover is good at nearly all aspects of MMA. Good - but not great. His time at the top is coming to an end sooner rather than later..

  • 5: Mauricio Hua

    25-10: While his title contending days may be over, Shogun does not fail to attain viewers.

  • 6: Volkan Oezdemir

    14-1: All the questions surrounding this man will be answered on July 29th, the date which he is set to face Jimi Manuwa.

  • 7: Corey Anderson

    9-3: With recent loses to Manuwa and Shogun, Anderson has failed twice to achieve that 'big-name' victory.

  • 8: Ovince Saint Preux

    20-10: Once considered to be on the verge of a title-shot, OSP has tumbled, going 1-3 in his last 4 fights, and only 2-4 in his last 6.

  • 9: Ilir Latifi

    12-5: He may not be an elite fighter, but Latifi has more than proved to be at least top-10 worthy.
