WELTERWEIGHT

    A change of scenery since the GSP days, 170 no longer hosts the likes of Johnny Hendricks, Rory Macdonald, Nick Diaz and Hector Lombard in the top 10. Instead, a newer and perhaps fresher breed of fighters have risen to fill the voids left behind.

  • (C): Tyron Woodley

    17-3: After defeating the man many thought was an uncrowned king, Woodley has cemented his place as the best current welter weight in the world. He very well could hold the best for a long, long time..

  • 1: Stephen Thompson

    13-2: He has already fought for the belt twice and came up short both times. So, what's next? The best thing Thompson can do for now is stay active and jump-start another win streak.

  • 2: Demian Maia

    26-5: One of the best submission artists in the UFC. The question is though, can Maia actually pose a challenge to the champ, who is nearly impossible to take down.

  • 3: Robbie Lawler

    27-11: Lawler has been in many wars over the past several years, so the time off after getting brutalized by Woodley is a very smart move.

  • 4: Jorge Masvidal

    32-12: Masvidal has a superior stand-up game and excellent wrestling defense. A title-shot in the near future is definitely a solid possibility.

  • 5: Neil Magny

    19-5: Magny's height - 6'3" - makes him the tallest welterweight on the roster. If he can learn to properly utilize his physical advantages on a consistent basis, he could become a legit title threat.

  • 6: Kelvin Gastelum

    13-2: Had it not been for poor dieting, many believe Gastelum would have already fought for the title. Better late than never.

  • 7: Carlos Condit

    30-10: Is Condit capable of a late career ressurection? Maybe he just needs to take some time off..

  • 8: Donald Cerrone

    32-8: Cerrone has always been a top fighter throughout his career who has had a tendency to crack under the big spot-light.

  • 9: Dong Hyun Kim

    22-3: Currently riding a 3 fight win streak, Kim leaves little doubt that he is a top 10 welter weight.
