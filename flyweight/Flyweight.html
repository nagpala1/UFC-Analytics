FLYWEIGHT

    The dominance of the champ gives out the illusion of the division being 'weak'. The statistics however, will prove otherwise. The best two are miles ahead of the rest, and the alpha is miles ahead of the beta.

  • (C): Demetrious Johnson

    26-2: Perhaps the best to ever do it. His salary wouldn't be able to back up that claim though..

  • 1: Joseph Benavidez

    25-4: Easily the second best fighter in the division. No shame in that.

  • 2: Henry Cejudo

    10-2: Cejudo is a dominant fighter who should stay in the top 5 for quite some time.

  • 3: Ray Borg

    11-2: Borg is making a title push. While an excellent fighter, it does not appear he has the tools to cause much of a challenge to the current champ.

  • 4: Wilson Reis

    22-7: Some believed Reis would be the one to dethrone the flyweight king. However, as evident by their recent fight, he simply wasn't.

  • 5: Jussier Formiga

    19-5: Formiga is a very solid gatekeeper. In fact, everyone in the division - sans the champ - is a gatekeeper if you really think about it..

  • 6: Sergio Pettis

    15-2: Although he's not as dominant as his bigger brother once was, Sergio definitely is the better fighter between the two at the current moment, with a ton of potential still remaining.

  • 7: Brandon Moreno

    14-3: Contender or pretender? Moreno's upcoming bout against Pettis will tell us all we need to know..

  • 8: Tim Elliott

    15-7: Elliot does not have enough to hang with the elite guys in the division.

  • 9: Ian McCall

    13-5: Ian "Uncle Creepy" McCall is almost as bad of a fighter as his nickname. Jokes aside, most of his loses were to great fighters, so he's actually pretty solid.
