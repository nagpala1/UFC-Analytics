STRAWWEIGHT

    The most stacked womens division of them all, the strawweights are a mighty bunch. The champ may be invincible, but rest are tight as nails..

  • (C): Joanna Jedrzejczyk

    13-0: Joanna has already cleaned out the majority of the division. Due to the level of competition she has faced, JJ has now surpassed Ronda Rousey in terms of dominance in the eyes of many.

  • 1: Claudia Gadelha

    15-2: Clearly the number 2 fighter at strawweight, Claudia has the best wreslting in the entire division. She must improve her cardio if she is to dethrone the champ.

  • 2: Karolina Kowalkiewicz

    10-2: Karolina has only lost 2 times in her career, which can be accredited to the two women above her in these rankings. No shame in that..

  • 3: Rose Namajunas

    7-3: Rose has improved drastically since her early days, and may be destined for a title-shot with one more victory.

  • 4: Jessica Andrade

    16-5: Jessica is an elite fighter, but she just doesn't have the tools to truly threaten the champ. Just goes to show you how good JJ really is..

  • 5: Tecia Torres

    8-1: It's hard to tell whether Tecia is truly a contender or just another pretender. But what isn't hard to tell, is her skill level. She is really, really good.

  • 6: Michelle Waterson

    14-5: Waterson is capable of throwing some really mean kicks, but outside of that her standup is dreadfully average.

  • 7: Joanne Calderwood

    11-2: Every division needs a gatekeeper, why not Calderwood for the strawweights?

  • 8: Carla Esparza

    12-4: You should always live in the moment, but a $1000 ice-cream sundae isn't the smartest investment one can make..

  • 9: Randa Markos

    7-5: Markos has a ton of guts for publicly calling out Cyborg. If only her skill level was as strong as her guts..
