MIDDLEWEIGHT

    Once ruled by Anderson Silva, the middle weight division has officially moved on. With the current depth, talent and killer instinct at 185, it may be hard for many to take into account that the current champ is the same man who was once a mere gate keeper for many long years in a past, weaker era of middle weights.

  • (C): Michael Bisping

    30-7: Surprise surprise. Although his first title defense - 40-sum year old Dan Henderson - wasn't the most impressive, the victory will nonetheless help to establish his legacy.

  • 1: Yoel Romero

    12-1: Romero is the most athletic and explosive fighter currently in the UFC. His interim-title fight against Robert Whittaker in July could very well be the most intriguing match-up all year.

  • 2: Robert Whittaker

    18-4: Whittaker's aggressive striking game and excellent wrestling defense makes him one of the deadliest fighters at 185.

  • 3: Gegard Mousasi

    42-6: 5 straight victories have placed Mr. Mousasi amongst the very best middle weights in the world. He once lacked killer instinct, but that appears to no longer be the case.

  • 4: Luke Rockhold

    15-3: Luke has gone MIA since getting besmirched by Bisping in June of last year. His age, skill and fight IQ are all in favor of another title-run, but his desire and will to fight are not.

  • 5: Jacare Souza

    24-5: Jacare is a top middleweight, but age may finally be catching up with the old croc.

  • 6: Chris Weidman

    13-2: 2 years ago there were serious debates about who would win a potential match-up between Chris Weidman and Jon Jones. I think we all know how that fight would turn out now..

  • 7: Kelvin Gastelum

    13-2: Is he a middle weight, or he is a welter weight? The fact is, Gastelum could be a top guy at both 185 and 170 if he can keep his eating habits in check.

  • 8: Anderson Silva

    34-8: The greatest middleweight of all time is still competing. At the age of 42, he is not only hanging around the top 10, but is beating some very good fighters in the process.

  • 9: Derek Brunson

    16-5: Once a promising contender, Derek has suffered 2 straight setbacks. What he needs more than anything is a little break from top-notch competition.
