BANTAMWEIGHT

    The womans bantamweight division has officially moved on from the Ronda Rousey era. The top-2 fighters are neck-and-neck in terms of their capabilities, and they are worlds apart from the rest of the women in the divison.

  • (C): Amanda Nunes

    14-4: If Shevchenko is unable to beat her, Nunes could stay champ for a long, long time...

  • 1: Valentina Shevchenko

    14-2: Valentina is easily the most well rounded fighter in the division, and could very well be the best as well. We will find out soon..

  • 2: Julianna Pena

    9-3: Pena has an extraordinary wresting and Jui-Jitsu game, but she doesn't do anything else all that well.

  • 3: Holly Holm

    10-3: Are Holm's best days behind her? Having lost her last 3 fights, Holly better snap out of this funk quick!

  • 4: Ronda Rousey

    12-2: Once considered as the most dominant athlete in the history of sports, Rousey turned out to be nothing other than a one-trick pony.

  • 5: Raquel Pennington

    9-6: After a slow start to her career, Pennington has turned up the heat and has established a niche at 135. At this point, she may just be the 3rd best fighter in the division.

  • 6: Sara Mcmann

    11-3: Mcmann has the capabilities of becoming a legit threat to the title. She just needs to put all of the pieces together on a more consistent basis.

  • 7: Cat Zingano

    9-2: Having suffered 2 losses in a row, the best days of Zingano's fighting career may be behind her..

  • 8: Liz Carmouche

    10-5: Carmouche won't be contending for the title anytime soon, but that doesn't mean she isn't a good fighter.

  • 9: Alexis Davis

    18-7: Alexis has proved her worth of being a top-10 fighter, but she just cannot beat the elite.
